## **KONU** BACK TO THE PAST

### **WISH CLAUSE**

Wish (if only) + the simple past tense

> to talk about something that we want to be different in the present or the future. We use the past tense but the meaning is present or future.

I wish (if only) I lived in a house by the sea. (I don't live in a house by the sea)

I wish you would stay with us longer. (but you won't stay with us longer)

**Note**: In formal English we use WERE instead of WAS with all subjects.

![](_page_0_Picture_9.jpeg)

I wish I **were** a good basketball player.

Wish (If only) + the past perfect tense

➤ to say that we regret something in the past.

I wish I had had enough courage to write you before. (But I didn't)

I wish I hadn't eaten so much last night. (But I ate.)

#### **IF CLAUSE TYPE 3**

We use third conditional for imaginary situation in the past, regret and criticism.

#### would have

If + past perfect, could have + past participle

## might have

If they had seen Tom, they would have told me. (But they didn't)

If you had studied harder, you wouldn't have failed the exam. (Criticism)

If he had saved enough money, he would have bought a bigger house.

If Pablo had installed some antivirus software, his computer wouldn't have got a virus.
