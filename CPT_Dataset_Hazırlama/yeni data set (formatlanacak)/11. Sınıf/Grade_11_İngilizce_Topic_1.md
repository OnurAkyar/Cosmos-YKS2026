### **The Future Tenses**

We use be going to

· for our plans.

I booked a table for two for this evening. I am going to take my wife to one of the best restaurants in the city.

· for intentions.

I'm going to study hard and pass the exam.

• for predictions based on what we see.

Look at these black clouds. It's going to rain.

![](_page_0_Picture_21.jpeg)

#### We use will future

for the decisions made at the time of speaking.
A: What would you like to have, coffee or tea?
B: I'll have tea, please. (The speaker decides at the moment of speaking.)

for predictions with no evidence.
 I don't know what I'll do tomorrow. I think I'll stay at home.

for promises or threats.

Take your umbrella with you; otherwise, you will get soaked.

Don't worry mum, I will let you know when I arrive.

 $\boldsymbol{\cdot}$  for requests and offers.

Will you please help me to tidy my room?

 with be sure, think, hope, be afraid, expect, believe, promise, guess maybe and perhaps.
 I hope I'll win the game.

I'm afraid she won't come to your party.

## Time expressions often used with the future tenses *Will* and *Be going to*:

tomorrow, tomorrow morning / afternoon / evening, next Monday / April / week / weekend / month / year, soon, in 2025.

## **KONU FUTURE JOBS**

### Making arrangements / appointments:

Are you free / available on Monday / today / at 2 p.m. / next weekend?

Can / Could we meet this evening / tomorrow afternoon / at 10 a.m.?

**Would you like to meet / come** next Friday / at weekend / at 5.30?

**How about meeting** next weekend / on Saturday morning?

When would be convenient for you? Saturday or Sunday?

What time would you like to meet?

Shall we arrange a meeting on Tuesday afternoon / meet for a coffee tomorrow?

I'd like to make an appointment to see Dr. Smith.

#### Accepting an arrangement / appointment:

Sure, I'll come.

Yes, Sunday is fine.

Friday would be perfect.

I'll be there at that time.

Yes, with pleasure.

I'd love to.

I think it's a great idea.

## Rejecting and cancelling an arrangement / appointment:

Sorry. I'm busy that day

I'm afraid. I can't come.

I'm sorry. I won't be able to make it on Saturday.

Sorry, but I don't think I can make it.

I'm afraid I can't. Maybe next time.
