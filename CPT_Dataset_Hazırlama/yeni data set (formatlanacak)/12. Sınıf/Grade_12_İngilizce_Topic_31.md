## **KONU** MANNERS - PART A

#### Talking about wishes and regret

- We express regret about a present state with a wish + past or continuous.
  I wish I were at school. (I'm not at school.)
- We express regret about the past with a wish +past perfect.
  I wish I hadn't forgotten my wallet at home. (I forgot my wallet at home.)
- We express a wish with wish + could.
  I wish I could swim. (I can't swim.)
- We use 'if only' instead of 'I wish' if the feeling is stronger.
  If only I hadn't been so sentimental. (I behaved so sentimentally. Now I regret it.)
- We use 'should have' or 'shouldn't have' to express regret about actions in the past.
  I shouldn't have counted on a phone call.
