THE VERB TO BE

We use the verb be

with a noun

My mother is a teacher.

• with an adjective.

This film is very interesting.

• with a prepositional phrase. The flowers are on the table.

## Affirmative: (+)

I am (I'm) a good student. You are (You're) a good student. He / She is (He' s / She's) a good student.

We / You / They are (We're / You're / They're ) good students.

It is (It's) a fast car.

## Negative: (-)

I am not (I'm not) a good student.

You are not(You aren't) a good student.

He / She is not (He/She isn't) a good student.

We / You / They are not(We / You/ They aren't) good students.

It is not (It isn't) a fast car.

Interrogative: (?)

Am I a good student? Yes, you are. / No, you aren't.

Are you a good student? Yes, I am. / No. I am not.

Is he / she is a good student? Yes, he/

she is. / No, he/she isn't.

Are you we / they good students? Yes, you/they are. / No, you/they aren't.

#### **SUBJECT PRONOUNS:**

. We use personal subject pronouns instead of nouns or names.

Heather is an opera singer. She is from Italy.
