#### **Wish Clauses**

We use wish (if only) + the simple past tense

• to talk about something that we want to be different in the present or the future. We use the past tense but the meaning is present or future.

There are a lot of things to do at work. I wish we didn't have to work today. (but there are a lot of things to do and we have to work today)

I wish I had enough money to buy that house. (I don't have enough money.)

I wish you would leave later. (but you won't leave later.)

**NOTE:** In formal English we use **WERE** instead of **WAS** with all subjects.

I wish I were a good basketball player.

We use wish (If only) + the past perfect tense

• to say that we regret something in the past.

I wish you had come earlier for the meeting. (but you didn't come early.)

I wish I hadn't drunk so much coffee during the day yesterday. (but I drank a lot of coffee.)

### We use wish (If only) + would

• when we want someone or something to change.

I wish that terrible noise would stop.

• when we want something to occur in the future.

Tina wishes her uncle would visit her soon.

• when we want to complain about something or other people's annoying habits.

I wish my son would stop biting his nails.

**NOTE:** To talk about our annoying habits, we use could (not would).

I wish I could be tidy.

#### **IF CLAUSE TYPE 3**

We use third conditional for imaginary, situation in the past, regret and criticism.

If + past perfect, would / could / might have + past participle

If she told me the truth, I wouldn't have got angry with her. (But she didn't)

If you had studied harder, you wouldn't have failed the exam. (critism)

• We can change the order of the clauses by busing comma:

He wouldn't have had the accident if he had been more careful.
