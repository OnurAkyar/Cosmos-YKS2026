### EXPRESSING LIKES / DISLIKES

We use like / love /enjoy to express positive feelings about something.

We use hate / dislike / don't - doesn't like to express negative feelings about something.

![](_page_0_Picture_22.jpeg)

We use the -ing form after verbs which express preference such as love, like, enjoy and hate.

### Examples:

She loves collecting teaspoons.

I like writing poems.

We can use 'quite or really' in our sentences. quite: totally, very really: very

### Examples:

I quite like playing chess. I really like rollerblading

Certainly, I prefer computers over tablets because I can only work with a big screen.

### **Talking about preferences**

- I prefer a mobile phone to/over a tablet.
- I prefer using digital mp3 player.
- · I'd prefer to watch a movie at home.
- I'd rather play computer games.

### Asking for preferences

- Do you prefer buying a tablet or a PC?
- Would you rather use a mouse or a touchpad?
- Which do you like better, TV or PC?
- · We can watch a movie or a TV series.
