# Modals used for obligations and necessity

#### MUST / MUSTN'T

We use must

• to express a strong obligation or necessity.

Mother to son: You **must** wash your hands before dinner.

¶ to express duty.

We must finish our project by Friday.

• to express very strong advice. This book is amusing. You must read it.

• when we are talking about rules and instructions.

You **must** wear a swimming cap in the pool.

#### We use mustn't

• when it is prohibited to do something- it's important or necessary not to do something.

You **mustn't** swim in that lake. It's forbidden

There will be an important meeting tomorrow. You mustn't be late.

#### **HAVE TO / HAS TO**

We use have to I has to

• to express necessity.

I have to wear glasses for reading.

• when there are obligations that come from outside the speaker.

Children have to start school at the age of 6. (It's the law.)

Jane has to write a composition until Friday. (The teacher said so.)

#### **DON'T / DOESN'T HAVE TO**

 We use don't I doesn't have to when there is no obligation to do something or when something is not necessary.

It's Saturday tomorrow, so I don't have to get up early.

The fridge is full of food. She doesn't have to go shopping.
