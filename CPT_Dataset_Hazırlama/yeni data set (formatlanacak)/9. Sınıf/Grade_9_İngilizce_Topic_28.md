### **KONU** INVITATIONS AND CELEBRATIONS - PART B

#### **COUNTABLE / UNCOUNTABLE NOUNS**

- Countable (C) nouns are things you can count. They can be singular or plural. e.g. one book, two books, three books.
- Uncountable (U) nouns are things you can't count. They can't be plural. e.g. water, flour; NOT two waters, three flour

**Note:** Some nouns can be either C or U but they have different meanings. **e.g.** Would you like some ice cream? (ice cream in general)

She wants an ice cream. (a cone of ice cream)

#### A / AN / SOME / ANY / A LOT OF

#### We use:

- a/an with singular C nouns.
- some with plural C and U nouns in affirmative.
- any with plural C and U nouns in negatives and questions.
- a lot of with plural C and U nouns in affirmative.

**Attention:** When we ask for or offer things, we can use some in questions.

e.g. Would you like some tea?Can I have some water?We usually use a lot of in affirmative.v

• We use *How much...*? with uncountable (U) nouns but *How many...*? with plural countable (C) nouns.
