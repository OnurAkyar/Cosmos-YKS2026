## **KONU** MODERN HEROES AND HEROINES

#### **Vocabulary:**

hero /'hɪərəʊ/ noun (C): a person, especially a man, who is admired by many people for doing something brave or good.

Einstein is the all-time **hero** of many scientists

Many people in the world think Superman is the most popular **hero**.

heroine /'herauɪn/ noun (C): a girl or woman who is admired because she has done something very brave or has achieved something great.

Halide Edip remains one of the **heroines** of the Turkish War of Independence.

My sister's favorite heroine is Storm, a member of the X-Men.

**Superpower**: an extraordinary power or ability.

When Ironman wears his suit, he has some superpowers such as superhuman strength and supersonic speed.

**Invisibility:** the fact or state of not being able to be seen

Invisible Woman is a heroine. Her superpower is invisibility.

#### **Talking about imaginary situations**

• We use Second Conditional sentences to talk about unreal or imaginary situations that are not likely to happen in the present or the future.

| if -clause               | main clause                            |
|--------------------------|----------------------------------------|
| if + the Past Simple     | would/could + base<br>form             |
| If you were more careful | you wouldn't make<br>so many mistakes. |

If I were a superhero, I would protect all the people in the world from the wicked.

• We can use **were** instead of **was** in the if clause of second conditional sentences.

If I had superpowers, I would use them to help people.

If I could fly, I wouldn't be late for my appointments.

You would lose some weight if you ate less bread.

If I had some free time, I'd travel abroad with my family.

If Tony were older, he could vote.
