# Simple Future Tense (WILL / WON'T)

When we make a sentence with Simple Future Tense, there is no plan or intention. Will / won't is used when we talk about a decision given at the moment of speaking.

| Affirmative   | I /You / | He / She / It / We / You / They          | will             | leave in 10 minutes. |
|---------------|----------|------------------------------------------|------------------|----------------------|
| Negative      | I /You / | He / She / It / We / You / They          | will not / won't | leave in 10 minutes. |
| Interrogative | Will     | I /You / He / She / It / We / You / They |                  | leave in 10 minutes? |

#### **Examples:**

- I will be very rich one day.
- Summer will come again.
- -She won't come with us because she is busy now.
- -Will you answer my question please? This is the third time I'm asking.
- -Everywhere is in a mess. What will you do now?
- These bags are heavy. I'll ask for help.

With the verbs 'think, hope, believe, be sure' and adverbs 'perhaps, probably' we also use will / won't.

- I'm sure they'll help me. (Eminim bana yardım edecekler.)
- He'll probably pass his class. (Muhtemelen sınıfını geçecek.)
- Perhaps she'll talk to me. (Belki de benimle konuşacak.)
- I think I'll win. (Sanırım kazanacağım.)
