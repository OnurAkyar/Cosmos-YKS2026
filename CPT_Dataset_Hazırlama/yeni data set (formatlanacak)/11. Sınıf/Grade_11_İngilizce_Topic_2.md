## **KONU** FUTURE JOBS – PART B

#### **Steps of job hunting:**

- 1. Research possible job opportunities.
- 2. Write or update your CV and letter of intent.
- 3. Send your application, letter of intent and CV.
- 4. Follow up your application and be sure it's been received.
- 5. Get ready to attend the interview.
- 6. Get the job.

### **Did you know?**

#### **A GAP YEAR**

In the professional world, a gap year is a year off between high school and college or university. Sometimes it is the break after graduating from university before starting the first job. It usually means travelling, volunteering or working abroad. It often means all three!

#### **The Future Continuous Tense**

**We use the future continuous tense**

**• to describe an unfinished action which will be in progress at a time in the future.**

We will be travelling across Europe by train in June. I can't come with you at 9 p.m. I will be watching the match at that time.

I'll be writing the articles for a tabloid newspaper next month.

**• to indicate that the longer action (the future continuous tense) will be interrupted by a shorter action in the future.**

When the guests arrive, we will be having dinner. **We use the present continuous tense for future**

**• for personal future arrangements.**

I am seeing Mary tomorrow.

He is taking Sue out for dinner on Sunday.
