# **HAVE GOT / HAS GOT**

| Subject             | have got ('ve got) / has got ('s got) | Object          |
|---------------------|---------------------------------------|-----------------|
| I / You / We / They | have got ('ve got)                    | a lavaly bausa  |
| He / She / It       | has got ('s got)                      | a lovely house. |

| Subject             | have not got (haven't got) /<br>has not got (hasn't got) | Object          |  |
|---------------------|----------------------------------------------------------|-----------------|--|
| I / You / We / They | have not got (haven't got)                               | a lavaly hausa  |  |
| He / She / It       | has not got (hasn't got)                                 | a lovely house. |  |

| Have /<br>Has | Subject             | got | Object          | Short Answers                                                                                                                                  |
|---------------|---------------------|-----|-----------------|------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Have          | I / you / we / they | got | a lovely house? | Yes, you have. / No, you haven't.<br>Yes, I have. / No, I haven't.<br>Yes, you have. / No, you haven't.<br>Yes, they have. / No, they haven't. |
| Has           | he / she / it       |     |                 | Yes, he has. / No, he hasn't.<br>Yes, she has. / No, she hasn't.<br>Yes, it has./ No, it hasn't.                                               |

- C) What do you do on Sundays?
- D) What's up?
- E) What are you doing?

**Cevap: A**

Jack is sixteen and Tom is thirteen. At home, we spend time playing computer games and doing our homework. My dad is a Maths teacher. He helps us when we don't understand at school.

### **What nationality is Karen's father?**

- A) Welsh
- B) Irish
- C) Spanish
- D) British E) Greek
